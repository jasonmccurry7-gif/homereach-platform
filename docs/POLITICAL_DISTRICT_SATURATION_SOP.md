# Political District Saturation SOP

Political District Saturation is a geography-only political mail planning service. It helps a campaign understand where to mail, what must be verified, what the likely production path looks like, and what approvals are needed before money, creative, or production move forward.

## Positioning

Sell this as campaign execution clarity, not voter surveillance.

- Good: district coverage, household counts, mail timing, creative readiness, production confidence.
- Avoid: voter tracking, ideology prediction, individual persuasion scoring, guaranteed votes, guaranteed wins.

## Price Basis

- Minimum planning package starts at $1,500.
- Mail, postage, maps, production, creative, rush timing, and extra drops are quoted after verified geography and counts.
- No checkout or production commitment should open until counts, disclaimer, quote, proof, and human approval are complete.

## Intake Requirements

Collect:

- Campaign or committee name.
- Candidate name.
- Office sought.
- Contact name, email, and phone.
- State.
- Geography level and geography value.
- District type.
- Election date.
- Preferred drop window.
- Desired drop count.
- Budget target.
- District/geography source.
- Target geography list.
- Audience basis.
- Disclaimer status.
- Compliance acknowledgements.

Target geography format:

```text
Name | Type | Source | Priority | Notes
District 7 | district | county board of elections | primary | verify boundaries before quote
```

## Fulfillment Workflow

1. Review intake and contact information.
2. Confirm race, office, election date, and geography.
3. Validate district boundaries and source.
4. Confirm route or household counts before quoting.
5. Confirm disclaimer requirements.
6. Confirm budget, drops, and delivery window.
7. Prepare geography-only campaign plan.
8. Prepare creative direction.
9. Route proposal, copy, proof, payment, and production through human approval.
10. Report aggregate delivery and response signals only.

## Readiness Scoring

The intake stores a readiness score based on:

- Geography captured.
- State/geography type/value present.
- Budget clears the minimum planning package.
- Drop count captured.
- Election timing.
- District source confirmed.
- Political compliance acknowledged.
- Sensitive targeting boundary acknowledged.
- Disclaimer status captured.
- No prohibited language detected.

## Deadline Rules

- More than 14 business days before election: feasible planning window.
- 6 to 14 business days: rush review.
- 5 or fewer business days: urgent feasibility review before proposal.
- Past election: decline production path or build post-election follow-up.

## Compliance Guardrails

Allowed:

- Geography-level targeting.
- District, county, city, ZIP, route, or campaign-provided audience planning.
- Public race context.
- Campaign-provided audience uploads where lawful and platform/vendor rules allow.
- Neutral campaign awareness messaging.
- Direct mail logistics, budget, timing, and route planning.

Not allowed:

- Individual voter belief classification.
- Ideology prediction.
- Persuasion scoring.
- Claims that HomeReach tracks voters.
- Guaranteed votes, turnout, wins, or results.
- Production without approved disclaimer, proof, quote, payment, and human approval.

## Reporting

Report only aggregate and known signals:

- Geography covered.
- Mail quantity.
- Drop dates.
- Delivery/in-home window.
- Proof status.
- Payment status.
- QR scans or landing page visits if available.
- Notes and recommended next drop.

Do not imply attribution certainty unless the campaign provides verified source data.
