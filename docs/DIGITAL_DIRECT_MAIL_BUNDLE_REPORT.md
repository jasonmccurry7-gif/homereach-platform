Status: Production operating guide and reporting template  
Owner: Revenue and Operations  
Applies to: Market Capture campaigns with direct mail add-on  
Last updated: 2026-05-30

## Purpose

This report defines how HomeReach sells, fulfills, and reports the Digital + Direct Mail bundle without adding new technical dependencies.

The bundle combines:

- Market Capture digital visibility
- Client-funded ad spend
- Optional targeted or shared postcard execution
- Simple campaign reporting
- Human-approved launch and production workflows

The goal is not to sell confusing ad-tech. The goal is to help a local business stay visible in the same neighborhoods through more than one channel.

## Client-Facing Positioning

Approved simple explanation:

```text
HomeReach helps your business stay visible in the neighborhoods that matter. We can run a Market Capture campaign online and pair it with postcards going to the same area, so homeowners see your business more than once.
```

Use:

- Local visibility
- Repeated exposure
- Neighborhood saturation
- Same-area digital and mail campaign
- Targeted campaign
- Postcard add-on
- Campaign tracking where available

Avoid:

- Guaranteed leads
- Guaranteed ROI
- We track every homeowner
- We follow people from mailbox to phone
- Spy on competitors
- Individual identity display
- Political belief targeting
- Attribution certainty when tracking is incomplete

## Offer Structure

Primary offer:

- Market Capture management: $499/month
- Ad spend: client-funded separately
- Direct mail: quote-based add-on
- Landing page: optional add-on
- Creative package: optional add-on

Recommended starter packaging:

| Package | Best For | Included |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Digital Only | First test or smaller budget | Market Capture setup, ad drafts, manual launch workflow, monthly report |
| Digital + Targeted Mail | Jobsites, neighborhoods, service areas | Digital campaign plus dedicated postcard drop to overlapping area |
| Digital + Shared Postcard | Lower-cost local visibility | Digital campaign plus protected shared postcard placement where available |
| Digital + Political Mail | Campaign or district awareness | Geography-based digital planning plus compliant political mail workflow |

Direct mail should remain quote-based until quantity, geography, print format, postage, and vendor cost are confirmed.

## When To Recommend The Bundle

Recommend Digital + Direct Mail when:

- The client has a clear target area
- The target area is valuable enough to justify repeated exposure
- The client has an offer, seasonal push, event, jobsite cluster, or campaign message
- The monthly ad budget alone is unlikely to create enough touchpoints
- The business benefits from trust and recall, such as roofing, HVAC, lawn care, landscaping, pest control, restaurants, med spas, dentists, real estate, and local political campaigns

Do not recommend the bundle when:

- The client expects guaranteed leads
- The target area is unclear
- Payment is not confirmed
- Assets are missing and timing is urgent
- Political compliance is uncertain
- Direct mail vendor cost would erase margin

## Operating Workflow

### 1. Intake

Use the existing Market Capture intake.

Confirm:

- Business name
- Contact details
- Industry
- Monthly ad budget
- Objective
- Targeting type
- Target area
- Campaign offer
- Postcard add-on interest
- Landing page need
- Creative package need
- Consent and compliance acknowledgement

If the client requests postcards, record the direct mail path as one of:

- Targeted Direct Mail
- Shared Postcards
- Political Mail
- Needs Quote
- Not Included

### 2. Payment And Quote

Before fulfillment:

- Confirm the $499/month Market Capture payment path
- Confirm client-funded ad spend separately
- Confirm direct mail is quoted separately unless a written bundle quote was approved
- Confirm landing page or creative package charges if applicable

Do not change pricing, charge a client, issue credits, or create subscriptions without human approval.

### 3. Campaign Planning

Create one combined campaign plan:

- Primary objective
- Target area
- Digital campaign angle
- Direct mail format
- Shared offer or message
- Landing page or tracking URL
- QR destination
- Launch schedule
- Mail drop schedule
- Reporting plan

The same offer does not need to appear word-for-word in both channels, but both should reinforce the same business outcome.

### 4. Creative Alignment

Digital creative and postcard creative should share:

- Business name
- Core offer
- Primary CTA
- Phone number or landing page
- Trust proof
- Visual identity
- Target area relevance

Check:

- Digital ad copy is platform-safe
- Postcard proof is print-safe
- QR destination works
- Tracking parameters are present where useful
- Political disclaimers exist where required
- No unsupported guarantees are present

### 5. Approval

Required before digital launch:

- Payment confirmed
- Ad spend confirmed
- Target area approved
- Creative approved
- Landing/tracking URL tested
- Admin approval recorded

Required before direct mail production:

- Payment or approved billing path confirmed
- Quantity and geography approved
- Final postcard proof approved
- Vendor quote approved
- Production approval recorded

Never auto-launch ads or release mail production without approval.

### 6. Launch

Digital campaign:

- Manual launch mode is default
- API draft mode may prepare assets only where enabled
- No paid ads launch automatically

Direct mail:

- Follow `docs/DIRECT_MAIL_FULFILLMENT_SOP.md`
- Track production status, mail/drop date, delivery window, and vendor confirmation

### 7. Monthly Reporting

Use this report to answer:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter?
- What should we do next?

Do not overwhelm clients with platform jargon.

## Internal Report Checklist

Before sending the report:

- Digital metrics are entered or marked unavailable
- Direct mail metrics are entered or marked unavailable
- Spend numbers match available platform or manual records
- Mail quantity and drop date are confirmed
- QR and landing page numbers are not double-counted without explanation
- Calls and leads are labeled as tracked, directional, or unavailable
- No guaranteed result language is included
- No individual-level targeting language is included
- Political reports stay geography-based and campaign-level
- Recommended next action is clear

## Measurement Rules

Use exact language:

- "Tracked responses"
- "Available reporting"
- "Directional signal"
- "Where available"
- "This does not capture every offline response"

Do not use:

- "Total sales generated" unless verified by client-provided sales data
- "Guaranteed ROI"
- "Every homeowner saw this"
- "Every click came from the postcard"
- "Every lead came from the campaign"
