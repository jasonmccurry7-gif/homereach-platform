# HomeReach Political Mail Execution Platform

## Ohio Voter Contact & Geographic Saturation Strategy

Prepared for: Dr. Amy Acton for Governor

This is a proposal deck. It does not imply campaign endorsement, approval, or authorization. Carrier-route IDs, route counts, postage, print cost, and checkout must be verified inside HomeReach Political Command before any production commitment.

## Slide 1 - Cover

Claim: HomeReach can help the campaign plan, price, creative, and execute a statewide postcard program with operational confidence.

Speaker notes: Position HomeReach as a campaign operations partner, not a print shop.

## Slide 2 - Who Is HomeReach

Claim: HomeReach turns mail into an operating system for statewide voter contact.

Proof: Operational stack showing intelligence, geography, creative, pricing, production, and tracking.

Speaker notes: Emphasize logistics-first thinking, route-level strategy, timing coordination, and visibility.

## Slide 3 - The Campaign Problem

Claim: Statewide campaigns lose time when mail strategy, creative, and execution live in separate rooms.

Proof: Pressure chart showing cost, speed, visibility, timing, and geographic precision.

Speaker notes: The problem is not postcards. The problem is disconnected execution.

## Slide 4 - The HomeReach Difference

Claim: HomeReach compresses quote, map, creative, approval, and mail execution into one visible workflow.

Proof: Traditional vendor versus HomeReach operating model.

Speaker notes: Show why the buying decision is about speed, control, and fewer handoffs.

## Slide 5 - Ohio Targeting Intelligence

Claim: Acton's strongest mail path starts with trust-building suburbs plus turnout-critical metro corridors.

Priority aggregate geographies:

- Franklin County suburban ring: Columbus, Dublin, Hilliard, Worthington, Upper Arlington, Westerville.
- Cuyahoga and Lake trust corridor: Cleveland, Parma, Lakewood, Shaker Heights, Mentor, Willoughby.
- Hamilton County suburban persuasion arc: Cincinnati, Norwood, Blue Ash, Sharonville, Anderson Township.
- Summit, Stark, and Mahoning opportunity zone: Akron, Cuyahoga Falls, Canton, Massillon, Youngstown.
- Lucas and northwest turnout amplifier: Toledo, Sylvania, Maumee, Oregon, Bowling Green corridor.
- Delaware, Union, and Warren growth corridors: Delaware, Powell, Marysville, Mason, Lebanon, Springboro.

Speaker notes: Keep this at aggregate geography level. Do not imply individual voter ideology scoring. Final carrier-route IDs require USPS EDDM or carrier-route data import.

## Slide 6 - Multi-Phase Campaign Strategy

Four proposed waves:

1. Name ID + Introduction
2. Trust + Healthcare Leadership
3. Contrast + Momentum
4. GOTV + Turnout Push

Speaker notes: Each wave has a distinct job, approval gate, quantity range, and in-home timing window.

## Slide 7 - Postcard Creative Engine

Claim: The AI creative layer produces complete front/back postcard concepts, not generic placeholder proofs.

Concept lanes:

- Emotional / Human
- Policy / Issue
- Trust / Social Proof
- Contrast / Urgency

Speaker notes: AI accelerates options. Campaign staff and legal review own final copy, claims, imagery, and disclaimers.

## Slide 8 - Operational Visibility

Claim: Campaign staff can see coverage, route readiness, creative status, and mail timing in one command view.

Speaker notes: This is the campaign command-center moment. The value is clarity under pressure.

## Slide 9 - Cost Predictability

Claim: Route-aware planning makes statewide mail budgets easier to forecast before final quote lock.

Speaker notes: Planning ranges are not final quotes. Checkout stays locked until route counts, print, postage, source timestamp, and approval are complete.

## Slide 10 - Why This Matters

Claim: The campaign gets more than mail; it gets disciplined message repetition across the state.

Speaker notes: Bring it back to campaign momentum, consistent story, and controlled execution.

## Slide 11 - Next Steps

Claim: Start with a focused strategy session, then convert the best route clusters into a pilot wave.

Next actions:

1. Campaign targeting review
2. Sample geography and route analysis
3. Creative direction review
4. Pilot wave discussion
